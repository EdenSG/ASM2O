Eden S.-G.

Surrealism

This piece represents how we can only really focus on one thing at a time. There is only one focal point, and everything else is out of focus, but the focus does not accurately follow the depth. I used the principle of balance to create a slightly lop-sided feel, emphasised by the curved lines and shapes. I created a washed-out, bright feel, showing the less-than-ideal range of thinking that happens when we try to focus on too much at once. The resistors in the background represent the hidden challenges that inevitably occur, and the ropes represent the ties that appear to hold our thinking together. The dogs show a playfulness that is sometimes necessary in order to “jump over” these challenges.

I created this compilation to explore the idea of focus, attention, and distraction by using familiar objects in unusual places.
